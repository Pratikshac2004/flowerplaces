5 Beautiful valleys in India that should be in your bucket list!

Tulip Garden, Kashmir

Frolic around among lush tulip fields at the largest Tulip garden in Asia! With various flower species like hyacinths, narcissus, daffodils, muscaria and iris paired with the show-stealer variety of tulips, this makes for one picturesque location in Kashmir. It overlooks the Dal lake and is surrounded by the Nishat Bagh and Chashma Shahi Mughal gardens. A visit to this place is sure to make your day bright and colourful!

Where: Srinagar, Kashmir
Best time to visit: March to April

Sunflower Field, Karnataka

Sunflower fields in Karnataka, especially around Haveri and Chitradurga, are known for their stunning landscapes creating a breathtaking sight..Haveri District known for its extensive sunflower fields, easily accessible from cities like Hubli and Dharwad.Chitradurga Offers beautiful views with sunflowers in the foreground and hills in the background. It's a beautiful and serene spot to relax and enjoy nature.

Where: Haveri and Chitradurga, Karnataka
Best time to visit: July to October

Kaas Plateau, Maharashtra

Kaas plateau is one of the beautiful places in Maharashtra. It gets its name from the Kaasa flower, which is the most common species found here. Along with that, this UNESCO world heritage site is home to more than 850 species of flowers including orchids, Indian arrowroot, Dipcadi flower, tropical sundew, Y-tura, toothbrush orchid and more. Various flowers bloom and close throughout the day on this plateau so you might witness different scenarios in the morning and evening.

Where: Satara district, Maharashtra
Best time to visit: Late August to September

Munnar Valley, Kerala

The Munnar Valley is most known for the rare flower of Neelakurinji that blooms once every 12 years. The valley gets covered in a lovely lavender lush in the bloom season. The great news is that the upcoming blossom season is this year, starting this month. So unless you’re in for another 12-year long wait, you should get to planning a trip to this beautiful vale!

Where: Idukki district, Kerala
Best time to visit: August to October

Valley Of Flowers, Uttarakhand

To get to the valley of flowers in Uttarakhand, you have to go through a 17km trek from Gobindghat village in Uttarakhand. This valley is part of the Nanda Devi Biosphere reserve which is one of UNESCO’s World Network of Biosphere Reserves. You find an immense diversity of flora here including flowers like the Himalayan maple, the blue Himalayan poppy, Brahmakamal, marigold, rhododendron, daisies, primulas, orchids and yellow, Jacquemont & Wallich varieties of cobra lilies. The Hemkund Gurudwara and the Nanda Devi national park are nearby tourist attractions that can be clubbed with a visit to this place.

Where: Gobindghat, Uttarakhand
Best time to visit: June to September
